"Populismo Descomplicado para o Popular"

"Populismo Descomplicado para o Popular" (Uncomplicated Populism for the Popular) is a short film made for the Argument and Narrative Structures
curricular unit of the Communication Sciences degree, and is based on the poem: "Hoje roubei todas as rosas dos jardins e cheguei ao pé de ti de mãos vazias."
(Today I stole all the roses from the gardens and came to you empty-handed.) by Eungénio de Andrade. The short then emerges from the interpretation of the poem
that the working group developed. We tried to explore a darker approach to social criticism, to reflect on contemporary society. The criticism would be how someone can
“use and “steal” others to reach a goal/get ahead in life, without offering them anything in return. At the same time, we wanted to represent that achieving a goal
in a dirty way results in the manipulative person losing their identity. To put this into practice, the group chose to tell the story of a populist politician who
manipulates and instrumentalizes the masses to achieve power, but as we didn't want to be completely literal we portrayed the story in a more symbolic and
theatrical way. We therefore create a guide to identify populism in politics through the politician/actor analogy.
The story then revolves around the attempt of an actor, initially unsuccessful, to achieve power and for this purpose, he steals identities, manipulates them
and creates a collective identity. He does this by imitating the audience and creating stereotypes about the people in it, leading them to believe and follow a common identity.
